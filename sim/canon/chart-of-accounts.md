# Chart of Accounts — Amalgamated Widget Holdings plc (Consolidated)

The Group maintains its books on a standard UK SME basis. All amounts are recorded in pounds sterling (£). The ledger enforces double entry in code: every journal entry must have total debits equal to total credits, or it is rejected and the rejection is logged. Fraud, when it occurs, is misclassification *within* a balanced ledger — never a broken entry.

Account types are one of: `asset`, `liability`, `equity`, `income`, `expense`. Assets and expenses are debit-normal; liabilities, equity and income are credit-normal.

## Notes on accounts of particular interest

- **1010 Freehold Property — Coventry Warehouse** and **7100 Impairment — Coventry Warehouse.** The carrying value of the Coventry warehouse is the Group's largest discretionary judgement. Impairments and reversals run through 7100 against 1010. "Supportable" is the operative word.
- **1300 Trade Debtors** vs **4000–4020 Widget Sales.** Receivables growing materially faster than revenue is the classic red flag and, accordingly, the classic temptation. The ratio of 1300 to revenue is watched by Internal Audit.
- **1400 Intercompany Receivable** / **2700 Intercompany Payable** / **4200 Licensing Income** / **6900 IP Licensing Expense.** The arrangements with Widgetco (Innovations) Ltd. The parent pays Innovations a licence fee for trademarks Innovations holds; Innovations' income is the Group's expense, and on consolidation these should eliminate. Whether they always do is a matter for the CFO.
- **2900 Deferred Income — WidgetCare™.** Cash received for service plans not yet performed. WidgetCare™ being principally an annual letter, the question of when this income is "earned" is more open than the Company lets on.
- **1600 Accumulated Depreciation** is carried as a contra-asset (credit balance within the asset class).
